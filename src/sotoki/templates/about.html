Basic Stack Exchange's knowledge

Stack Exchange's websites are social platforms where anybody can post a question related to the site's topic and anybody can write answers or comments to it. Users can Up-vote or Down-vote answers and comments in order to distinguish those that are most useful.

This voting gets computed into a per-question, per-answer, per-comment Score that is displayed thorough the website to help you skim to the most valuable content.

When the user who raised a question is satisfied with an answer, he may mark it as Accepted. There is now a green mark next to it to indicate that. The accepted answer can change overtime (in the online platform).

Users interacting with the platform (receiving Up-vote for their answer for instance) are attributed “rewards” that gets computed into a Reputation which is a per-user Score.

Additionnaly, Users receive badges depending on sub-topic specific actions. Badges are sorted in three levels: Gold, Silver and Bronze.
